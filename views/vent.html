by deadbyshruberry 4 days ago in depression

I don't know if anyone will agree with this, but it seems like, if you tell people you're depressed and anxious, at first they'll feel bad for you and try to help you, but then they start to say you just need to snap out of it, and then they start to think you're just craving attention (especially when you start thinking about suicide).

So then you stop telling your family and friends, you start pretending things are better, but you just feel worse and worse, and you keep feeling suicidal more and more, but you speak of it less and less. And then eventually, someone does commit suicide, when they have not told anyone they were about to.

And yet, based on what I've seen from when people have committed suicide, the general feeling is that "oh, if only X had told us how he/she felt, we could've helped them, we would've helped them".
